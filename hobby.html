01 — My Story

More Than Just a Sport

Soccer has been the single most constant thread running through my life. Before I knew what a career was, before I understood leadership or teamwork in any formal sense, I understood them on a pitch. I understood that the person next to you is counting on you, and that your job isn't to be the loudest player — it's to be the most reliable one.

Some of my closest friends today are people I met through soccer. There's something about playing together — grinding through pre-season, celebrating a win, picking each other up after a loss — that builds a kind of trust you can't manufacture anywhere else.

I captained the Lone Peak High School team, an experience that taught me how to lead people who are just as competitive as you are. It's a different kind of leadership than managing a team in a business context — it's immediate, physical, and deeply personal. Those lessons have followed me into every room I've walked into since.

At BYU, I brought that same mentality to intramural soccer and came away with a championship in 2025. The level may be different, but the joy of competing with people you care about never changes.

03 — Position & Style

Life at Center Back

Center back is the position that most people don't notice until something goes wrong. That suits me. My job is to read the game two plays ahead, organize the defensive line, and make sure the chaos in front of me never reaches the goalkeeper. When I do my job well, it's invisible.

Playing CB has sharpened my spatial awareness, my ability to stay calm under pressure, and my communication. You can't defend well in silence — you're constantly directing, warning, adjusting. It's a position that demands both physical composure and mental clarity at the same time.

My favorite CB in the world right now is Rúben Dias of Manchester City — a player who combines elite reading of the game with vocal leadership and a relentless work rate. I try to bring those same qualities every time I play.

  • Strength Reading the game and positioning
  • Strength Aerial duels and set pieces
  • Strength Organizing the defensive line
  • Role Model Rúben Dias — Man City
  • Philosophy The best CB is the one you forget is there
